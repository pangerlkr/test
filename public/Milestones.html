Institutional History

Milestones
& Landmarks

Two decades of institutional progress, landmark rulings, legislative influence, and community impact across Nagaland's hills.

2006
Founding Milestone

Commission Established by Act of Legislature

The Nagaland State Commission for Women Act, 2006 was enacted by the Nagaland Legislative Assembly, formally constituting the Commission with statutory powers to investigate, monitor, and advise on all matters relating to constitutional and legal safeguards for women.

2007
First Commission

Inaugural Chairperson Appointed

The first Chairperson and Members of the Commission were formally appointed by the State Government, marking the operational commencement of the body. The inaugural session set the institutional agenda for the first five-year mandate period.

2008
Research & Policy

First Customary Law Study Commissioned

The Commission initiated its first systematic study of customary laws relating to Naga women across tribes and districts — a foundational research effort that would shape policy recommendations for the next decade.

2010
Outreach Program

District Awareness Campaigns Launched

State-wide awareness campaigns were conducted across all 11 districts of Nagaland, educating women about their legal rights, grievance redressal mechanisms, and the Commission's services — reaching an estimated 25,000 women in rural and semi-urban areas.

2012
Custodial Rights

First Custodial Inspections Conducted

Under Section 5(9) of the Act, the Commission conducted its first formal inspections of jails, remand homes, and women's custody centers across Nagaland, submitting a landmark report to the State Government on conditions and recommended reforms.

2015
Legislative Impact

Recommendations Shape State Policy

The Commission's comprehensive review of Acts affecting women in Nagaland led to formal policy recommendations adopted by the State Legislature. Amendments to workplace protection provisions and land inheritance guidelines were directly influenced by the Commission's advisory submissions.

2018
Economic Empowerment

Women Entrepreneurship Initiative

Partnering with the Department of Commerce and Industries, the Commission launched a targeted initiative supporting women-led micro-enterprises in rural Nagaland, providing legal literacy training and access to formal financial channels for over 1,200 participants.

2020
Crisis Response

Pandemic Relief & Digital Literacy Drive

During the COVID-19 pandemic, the Commission pivoted its operations to address heightened domestic violence risks, establishing emergency helpline coordination with the Nagaland Police. Simultaneously, a digital literacy program was rolled out for rural women entrepreneurs across 6 districts.

2022
Socio-Economic

Grassroots Leadership Programme

The 2022-2023 Commission period focused on grassroots leadership — training women at the Village Council level in legal rights advocacy, culminating in the first ever NSCW-facilitated Women's Leadership Summit attended by delegates from all 11 districts.

2024
Present

Comprehensive Legal Aid & Outreach

The current Commission period focuses on a comprehensive review of legal aid delivery, community outreach expansion, and policy advocacy initiatives. The Annual Report 2023-2024 documents 124 policy actions taken and over 4,800 cases addressed across Nagaland's districts.
