Statutory Authority

Specific powers and operational functions as mandated by the Nagaland State Commission for Women Act, 2006.

ACT-2006

(1) While performing functions under section 5 of this Act, involving investigation and inquiry, the Commission shall have the powers of a civil court trying a suit and, in particular, in respect of the following matters:

  • record_voice_over Summoning and enforcing the attendance of any person from any part of India and examining him on oath.
  • text_snippet Requiring the discovery and production of any documents.
  • verified Receiving evidence on affidavits.
  • folder_shared Requisitioning any public record or copy thereof from any court or office.
  • history_edu Issuing Commissions for the examination of witnesses and documents.
  • more_horiz Any other matter which may be prescribed.

(2) The Commission may for the purpose of conducting investigations and inquiry under this Act, utilize the services of any officer of the State Government with its prior approval.

(3) The other procedural details in regard to conduct of investigations and inquiries under this Act shall, as far as possible, be the same as the procedure followed by a Civil Court.

According to Section 5, the Commission shall:

01

Study, research and codify customary laws relating to Naga women, family institutions, and social security across tribes and districts.

02

Study and report on the advisability of reorienting customary laws and practices into legislative enactments.

03

Research Acts of Parliament (CPC, CrPC, IPC) regarding their applicability to Naga women under article 371-A.

04

Analyze all Acts of the Nagaland Legislative Assembly affecting women to plug loopholes and improve effectiveness.

05

Advise on specific provisions in the Constitution of India for protecting the interests of women in Nagaland.

06

Formulate alternative policies for the socio-economic and educational advancement of women in Nagaland.

07

Examine and monitor the general conditions of women across the state.

08

Evaluate conditions for women in both private and public workplaces.

09

Inspect Jails, Remand Homes, and custody centers to ameliorate conditions against human rights violations.

10

Receive, investigate and report on complaints relating to the status and affairs of women.

11

Perform all other functions incidental to the empowerment and welfare of women.

(2) It shall also be the duty of the Commission to conduct special inquiries into matters affecting welfare and empowerment as referred by the State Government.
